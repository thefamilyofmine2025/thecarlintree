60. Lillian Catherine CARLIN [scrapbook] (John William , Joseph , Matthias , Peter ) was born 1 on 10 Apr 1867 in DAVIESS COUNTY KY. She died on 22 Mar 1963 in INDIANAPOLIS IN STELLA ALLISONS HOME. She was buried 2 in ROSE HILL CEMETARY, OWENSBORO, KY.

BIOGRAPHY: AT THE AGE OF FIVE (5)SHE WAS TAKEN BY HER PARENTS TO THE WEST. HER FATHER DIED BEFORE THEY REACHED THEIR DESTINATION. MASON'S IN THE AREA OF DENVER CO AID HER MOTHER IN RETURNING THE BODY TO KENTUCKY FOR BURIAL. HE IS BURIED IN THE CARLIN CEMETARY (FAMILY) ON THE OLD HENDERSON ROAD. MRS. T G BARTLETT, A CARLIN DESCENDENT NOW OWNS THE FARM TODAY (1976)

BIOGRAPHY: IN 1873 SUSAN CATHERINE AND HER CHILDREN RETURNED TO LIVE ON THE CARLIN FARM. SUSAN CARLIN DIED 22, 1882. SHE IS BURIED IN THE CARLIN GRAVEYARD (NO MARKER) AUNT LILLIE, LIKE THE GREGORYS ATTENDED PLEASANT GROVE CHURCH (BAPTIST) FOR MORE THAN THREE SCORE AND TEN YEARS. BEFORE HER MARRIAGE SHE STAYED WITH HER SISTER SALLIE CARLIN GREGORY. MEMOU SAID ON NUMEROUS OCCASIONS SHE SAVED WILLIAMS LIFE WHEN HE WAS A BABY. SHE WAS A SOURCE OF INSPIRATION, COMFORT AND HELP IN MEMOURS FAMILY. IN 1896 AUNT LILLIE MARRIED THADDEUS BELLEW IN DAVIES COUNTY.

BIOGRAPHY: AFTER THE DEATH OF HER HUSBAND SHE CONTINUED TO OPERATE THEIR FARM ON WEST 5TH STREET ROAD. SHE REMARRIED IN 1915 TO A MR. POWELL FROM SEBREE CO. HE DIED A FEW YEARS LATER.
TWO CHILDREN WHERE BORN TO LILLIE CARLIN & THADDEUS BELLEW. STELLA BORN IN 1898 AND MARTIN THADDEUS IN 1900
AUNT LILLIE HAD MANY PROBLEMS BUT NEVER SEEMED TO BE DISCOURAGED OR TO SHOW DISCOURAGEMENT. AT THE AGE OF 85 (1955) SHE LOST HER HOME BY FIRE. IT WAS REBUILT BY THE TIME SHE WAS 88.
SHE WAS AM ACTIVE CHEERFUL CHRISTAIN UNTIL THE TIME OF HER DEATH IN 1963. (AGE 96) SHE DIED AT THE HOME OF HER DAUGHTER MRS. JOHN ALLISON, STELLA HARRIS BELLEW) IN INDIANAPOLIS. HER MANY HOURS OF WALKING ALL OVER OWENSBORO ABOUT EVERY DAY PROBABLY CONTRIBUTED TO HER GOOD HEALTH AND LONGEVITY.

BIOGRAPHY: This information taken from a handwritten document given by Frances Magnetta Bellew Melhiser. Author unknown.
