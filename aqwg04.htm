50. John M CARLIN [scrapbook] (David F , Matthias , Peter ) was born in Jun 1848 in Elk Creek Spencer County KY. He died on 30 Dec 1887 in Rivals Spencer County KY. He was buried in Slone-Carlin Cemetery Normandy KY.

Son of David F. Carlin and Elizabeth McCrocklin, died at his father's home while waiting to take the Normandy train. John was born on his father's farm in 1848 and left the farm for Louisville in 1869 to pursue his city life ambitions. He began work as a clerk in a dry goods store at 21. He soon thereafter married his 2nd cousin Agnes Guthrie and the two lived in Owensboro KY by 1872. Both Agnes and their baby daughter Emma died shortly after Emma's birth in 1874. John had great ambition to start his own dry goods business in Owensboro, but times were hard and his business ventures failed.

By 1881, John had taken employment as a salesman for a merchant tailor (Harris & French) where he found success and gained loyal customers across a broad area from Kentucky to Missouri and as far south as Texas, soliciting orders for men's suits in various towns along the railways. In 1883 he met a widow named Laura Mickey whose first husband James DeMoss had died in 1880. John and Laura were married in April of 1884, and enjoyed their two sons, Fayette born in 1885, and Roy in 1886. By 1887, John had been diagnosed with liver cancer and died at his father's home December 30, 1887, while napping waiting to catch the Normandy train to Louisville.

John married Agnes GUTHRIE in 1873. Agnes was born in 1852. She died in 1875.

They had the following children.
